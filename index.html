Monkey: gym pet

Monkey's research

This page highlights the research themes that inspire how we build Monkey: Gym Pet — a gamified fitness app designed to encourage consistency, healthier habits, and long-term progress.

Why this exists

Monkey is built around a simple idea: healthy habits compound. Regular movement, strength training, better sleep, and a balanced diet are all linked to better physical health, improved mood, more energy, sharper thinking, and long-term well-being.

We use these ideas to shape a fitness experience that is playful, motivating, and rooted in evidence — not gimmicks.

1) Movement improves more than just fitness

Credible health organizations consistently show that physical activity helps people feel better, function better, and sleep better. It is also associated with lower anxiety, improved cardiovascular health, and reduced risk of many chronic diseases.

  • Exercise improves mood and mental health.
  • Regular activity improves cardiovascular health.
  • Physical movement improves sleep quality and cognitive performance.

Sources: CDC – Health Benefits of Physical Activity, Harvard Health – Exercise & Brain Function

2) Strength training matters

Resistance training is one of the most well-supported forms of exercise for improving muscular strength, muscle mass, metabolic health, and long-term mobility.

  • Progressive resistance training increases muscle hypertrophy.
  • Strength training improves metabolic health and body composition.
  • Structured training programs help create sustainable progress.

Sources: American College of Sports Medicine – Resistance Training

3) Recovery and sleep are part of training

Recovery is not optional. Sleep plays a critical role in muscle recovery, hormone regulation, mental performance, and overall health.

  • Sleep supports muscle repair and recovery.
  • Sleep influences cognitive performance and mood.
  • Sleep restriction can impact hormone balance including testosterone.

Sources: NIH – Why Sleep Matters, JAMA – Sleep Restriction & Testosterone

4) Nutrition fuels performance

Training outcomes depend heavily on nutrition. A balanced diet consisting of whole foods, quality protein sources, healthy fats, and nutrient-dense carbohydrates supports physical performance and long-term health.

  • Whole foods support sustained energy.
  • Protein supports muscle growth and repair.
  • Balanced diets improve long-term health outcomes.

Sources: Harvard T.H. Chan – Healthy Eating Plate

Healthy habits support a healthier life

The broader philosophy behind Monkey is that fitness is not just about aesthetics. Physical activity, sleep, and nutrition together help people feel stronger, think clearer, improve confidence, and live healthier lives.

  • Exercise improves long-term physical health.
  • Healthy habits improve mood and resilience.
  • Consistency compounds into meaningful long-term results.
