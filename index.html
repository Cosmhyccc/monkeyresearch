Monkey: gym pet

Monkey's research

This page highlights the research themes that inspire how we build Monkey: Gym Pet — a gamified fitness app designed to encourage consistency, healthier habits, and long-term progress.

Why this exists

Monkey is built around a simple idea: healthy habits compound. Regular movement, strength training, better sleep, and a balanced diet are all linked to better physical health, improved mood, more energy, sharper thinking, and better long-term well-being. We use these ideas to shape a fitness experience that is playful, motivating, and rooted in evidence — not gimmicks.

1) Movement improves more than just fitness

Credible health organizations consistently show that physical activity helps people feel better, function better, and sleep better. It is also associated with lower anxiety, better cardiovascular health, and lower risk for multiple chronic diseases.

  • The CDC notes that physical activity has both immediate and long-term benefits, including better sleep, less anxiety, and better overall health.
  • Harvard Health highlights that exercise can improve mood and support memory and thinking, partly by helping with stress and sleep.

Sources: CDC – Health Benefits of Physical Activity for Adults, CDC – Benefits of Physical Activity, Harvard Health – Exercise can boost memory and thinking skills, Harvard Health – Regular physical activity can boost mood

2) Strength training matters

Monkey is not just about random activity. It is designed around purposeful training. Resistance training is one of the most evidence-backed ways to improve muscular fitness, physical function, and body composition.

  • The American College of Sports Medicine has long emphasized progressive resistance training as a core part of fitness programming.
  • Their position stand describes progression over time, and for hypertrophy-focused training it recommends structured resistance training, progressive overload, and higher-volume multiple-set programs.

In practice, that means the app should reward consistency, real effort, and gradual improvement — not shortcuts.

Sources: ACSM – Position Stands, ACSM Position Stand – Progression Models in Resistance Training for Healthy Adults

3) Recovery and sleep are part of the training plan

A lot of people think results come only from grinding harder. The research says otherwise. Sleep is not optional maintenance — it is part of performance, recovery, cognition, and health.

  • The NIH explains that sleep supports healthy brain function and physical health, and that insufficient sleep raises risk across multiple body systems.
  • Sleep also affects energy, focus, memory, mood, learning, and decision-making.
  • Research in healthy young men found that one week of sleep restriction reduced daytime testosterone levels, which matters because sleep plays an important role in healthy hormonal function.

That is why Monkey treats recovery as part of getting stronger, not as laziness.

Sources: NIH/NHLBI – Why Is Sleep Important?, NIH/NHLBI – How Sleep Works, JAMA – Effect of 1 Week of Sleep Restriction on Testosterone Levels in Young Healthy Men

4) Food quality matters

Exercise works better when it is paired with a balanced diet. We are inspired by evidence-based nutrition principles that focus on whole, minimally processed foods and sustainable eating habits rather than extremes.

  • Harvard’s Healthy Eating Plate emphasizes vegetables, fruits, whole grains, healthy protein sources, and healthy oils.
  • It also encourages limiting sugary drinks and being mindful about food quality, not just calories.

For Monkey, this means promoting habits that support performance, recovery, and long-term health — not crash diets or unrealistic body ideals.

Sources: Harvard T.H. Chan – Healthy Eating Plate, Harvard T.H. Chan – Healthy Eating Pyramid

5) Healthy habits support a healthier, happier life

The broader idea behind Monkey is that fitness is not only about appearance. Good habits can help people feel more capable, more stable, and more satisfied in daily life.

  • Physical activity is linked to better mood, lower anxiety, and better day-to-day functioning.
  • Better sleep supports mood, focus, creativity, learning, and decision-making.
  • Strength training and sound nutrition support physical resilience and long-term health.

We want Monkey to make these habits easier to stick to by turning consistency into something visible, rewarding, and fun.

Sources: CDC – Health Benefits of Physical Activity for Adults, Harvard Health – Regular physical activity can boost mood, National Institute on Aging – Why Sleep Is Important

How this research shapes Monkey

  • Consistency over perfection: we want to reward repeated healthy actions, because long-term results come from repetition.
  • Training + recovery: workouts matter, but so do sleep, rest, and nutrition.
  • Progressive growth: the app is inspired by the idea that gradual overload and habit-building drive real adaptation.
  • Mood and motivation: fitness should feel encouraging, not punishing.
  • Whole-person health: we care about energy, confidence, resilience, and overall well-being — not just aesthetics.
